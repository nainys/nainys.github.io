Introduction

Hate speech can be defined simply as language that is used to expresses hatred towards a targeted individual/group or is intended to be derogatory, to humiliate, or to insult the members. Traditional methods for hate speech detection use tons of training data to mine the hateful structure but due to disproportionate use of different terms they are prone towards learning bias against specific objects, personalities or groups. Idea is to propose a method that takes into account the grammatical structure of the sentence to predict hatefulness. We extend two structure based approaches from different fields - Sentiment Analysis and Rumor Detection, into the domain of hatespeech detection and see how well structure based models work for hatespeech detection.

Approach 1

In this approach we used Standford's Recursive Deep Models for Semantic Compositionality Over a Sentiment Treebank

This approach involves training a complicated recursive neural network called as Recursive Neural Tensor Network that uses tensors, rather than standard matrices, as parameters.
Recursive Neural Tensor Networks takes as input phrases of any length. It represents a phrase through word vectors and a parse tree and then compute vectors for higher nodes in the tree using the same tensor-based composition function.

Approach 2

This approach is based on Rumor Detection on Twitter with Tree-structured Recursive Neural Networks (RvNN)

The input into our model is a propagation tree rooted from a source post and each tree node is a responsive post instead of an individual words. The content semantics of posts and the responsive relationship among them can be jointly captured via the recursive feature learning process along the tree structure.

The core idea of the method is to strengthen the high-level representation of tree nodes by the recursion following the propagation structure over different branches in the tree.

Variants

We tried the model on the following variants -

Bag of words
- The baseline model utilizes a bag of words representation. This serves as a reference point for us.
Word Embeedings
- Word at a leaf node are represented using word embedding trained over a Twitter Corpus.
